## Why this course exists

You are going to become a **professional data analyst**. Not a button-pusher, not a maker of pretty-but-empty dashboards: an analyst who understands what the numbers mean, who knows when to trust them, and who knows how to tell their story.

For that, this course deliberately mixes:

- Mathematics, statistics, code, tools
- Philosophy of science, logic, ethics
- Writing, rhetoric, literature
- A little physics, a little political science

This wide view — sometimes called an "Oppenheimer view" — is not decoration. The best analysts are not the ones who know the most Python libraries: they are the ones who can **ask the right question** and **doubt their own results honestly**.

## How your brain learns

Three facts, established by learning-sciences research, will guide the whole course:

1. **Retrieval causes learning, not re-reading.** Testing yourself (even failing) carves things into memory. Re-reading makes you *feel* you know without actually knowing.
2. **Spacing beats cramming.** Reviewing a little each day for two weeks is several times more effective than one big session the night before — for the same total time.
3. **Interleaving beats massed practice.** Mixing exercise types is more effective than repeating the same type twenty times in a row.

This course applies these three principles automatically. You don't have to worry about them — but you do have to **trust the process**, even when it feels harder than passive re-reading. The fact that it feels harder *is exactly what makes it work*.
